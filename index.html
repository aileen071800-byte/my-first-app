Introduction

About Me

Hello, I'm Jooeun Lim — a dance student at Sungkyunkwan University (SKKU) in Seoul, South Korea, with a deep passion for movement as both art and inquiry.

My practice bridges classical and contemporary forms, exploring how the body communicates what words cannot. I am drawn to the intersection of choreography, culture, and identity — investigating how dance serves as a living archive of human experience across nations and generations.

Beyond performance, I pursue research that situates dance within broader sociocultural contexts, asking questions about globalisation, artistic exchange, and the transformative power of movement in public life.

I am committed to growing as a versatile artist — in the studio, on stage, and in the academic study of dance — and to contributing to the vibrant landscape of Korean and international contemporary dance.

Research · Global Dance Studies

Analysis of Overseas Tour Countries

A comprehensive study examining the international touring patterns of Korean dance companies and individual artists. This project maps the countries most frequently visited, investigates the cultural and economic factors shaping these itineraries, and reflects on how overseas tours influence artistic development, cross-cultural exchange, and the global dissemination of Korean contemporary dance.
